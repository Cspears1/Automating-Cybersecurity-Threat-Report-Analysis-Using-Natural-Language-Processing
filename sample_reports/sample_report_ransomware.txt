A healthcare organization reported a major service disruption after several employees opened a document that appeared to be a routine invoice from a medical supply vendor. Shortly after the attachment was opened, multiple shared folders became unavailable, and users reported that patient scheduling files, billing records, and internal documents could no longer be accessed. The affected files were renamed with unusual extensions, and a payment note appeared on several systems demanding money in exchange for file recovery.

Security analysts identified outbound traffic from 91.203.18.55 and communication attempts to backup-update-service.net. During the investigation, the team discovered that the malware attempted to locate backup directories, delete recovery copies, and disable restore options before encrypting files. Several endpoints also showed signs of lateral movement, unauthorized account activity, and attempts to access administrative shares. A suspicious payload hash was recorded as 5f4dcc3b5aa765d61d8327deb882cf99.

The report also mentions possible exploitation of CVE-2023-34362 during the intrusion, which may have allowed attackers to gain initial access before moving to other systems. Administrators found evidence that files were encrypted across multiple departments and that backup recovery paths were modified. The security team should isolate affected systems, block the domain and IP address, verify offline backups, review endpoint logs, rotate credentials, and begin incident response procedures before restoring systems.
